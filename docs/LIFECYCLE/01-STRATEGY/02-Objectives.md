# Objectives

The Strategy stage establishes the business foundation for the Enterprise Network Architecture Framework (ENAF). Its objectives are focused on understanding the organization, defining the transformation vision, and ensuring that architecture activities remain aligned with business priorities.

By the end of this stage, architects and business stakeholders should have a shared understanding of why the initiative exists, what it aims to achieve, and how success will be measured.

---

## Primary Objectives

The Strategy stage has the following primary objectives.

### Understand the Business Vision

Gain a clear understanding of the organization's long-term vision, mission, and strategic direction.

Architecture should support where the business wants to go, not simply improve the current technology landscape.

---

### Identify Business Drivers

Understand the factors driving the transformation.

Business drivers may include:

- Business growth
- Digital transformation
- Cloud adoption
- Mergers and acquisitions
- Regulatory requirements
- Security improvements
- Cost optimization
- Customer experience
- Operational efficiency
- Technology modernization

Understanding these drivers ensures that architectural decisions address real business needs.

---

### Define Business Objectives

Identify the measurable business outcomes that the initiative is expected to deliver.

Examples include:

- Improve service availability
- Reduce operational costs
- Increase business agility
- Enable cloud transformation
- Strengthen cybersecurity
- Improve employee productivity
- Support global expansion

Business objectives should be clear, realistic, and measurable.

---

### Define the Scope

Establish the boundaries of the initiative.

The scope should clearly define:

- Business units involved
- Geographic coverage
- Technologies affected
- Business capabilities included
- Major assumptions
- Known constraints

A clearly defined scope helps prevent uncontrolled expansion during the project lifecycle.

---

### Identify Stakeholders

Identify everyone who has an interest in the success of the initiative.

Typical stakeholders include:

- Executive sponsors
- Business leaders
- Enterprise architects
- Network architects
- Security architects
- Cloud architects
- Operations teams
- Project managers
- Business users

Understanding stakeholder expectations improves communication and decision-making throughout the lifecycle.

---

### Define Success Criteria

Agree on how success will be measured.

Success criteria may include:

- Business outcomes achieved
- Budget adherence
- Delivery timelines
- Service improvements
- Security improvements
- Customer satisfaction
- Operational efficiency
- Compliance objectives

Success criteria should be agreed upon before architecture work begins.

---

### Identify Risks and Constraints

Understand the factors that may influence the success of the initiative.

Examples include:

- Budget limitations
- Resource availability
- Regulatory obligations
- Existing technology constraints
- Business dependencies
- Organizational readiness
- Skills availability

Identifying these factors early allows them to be considered throughout the architecture lifecycle.

---

### Align Business and Architecture

Ensure that architecture activities directly support business priorities.

At this stage, architects should confirm that:

- Every architectural objective supports a business objective.
- Every major investment has business justification.
- Technology decisions will be driven by business value.

Business alignment is one of the fundamental principles of ENAF.

---

## Expected Results

The Strategy stage is considered complete when the following outcomes have been achieved:

- Business vision is understood.
- Business drivers are documented.
- Business objectives have been defined.
- Project scope has been agreed.
- Stakeholders have been identified.
- Success criteria have been established.
- Risks and constraints have been documented.
- Executive sponsorship has been confirmed.
- Business and architecture are aligned.

These outcomes provide the foundation for the Current State stage.

---

## Key Questions

Before progressing to the next stage, architects should be able to answer the following questions:

- Why is this initiative required?
- What business problem are we solving?
- What outcomes does the business expect?
- Who are the key stakeholders?
- What is included in scope?
- What is outside the scope?
- How will success be measured?
- What constraints must be respected?
- What risks have already been identified?

If these questions cannot be answered confidently, additional strategy work should be completed before proceeding.

---

## Summary

The objectives of the Strategy stage are to establish a shared understanding of the business, define the purpose and scope of the initiative, and align architectural activities with organizational goals.

Completing these objectives ensures that the remainder of the ENAF lifecycle is guided by business value rather than technology preferences.
