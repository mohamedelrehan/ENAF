# Purpose

The Strategy stage is the starting point of the Enterprise Network Architecture Framework (ENAF). It establishes the business context for every architecture decision that follows and ensures that technology investments are driven by business needs rather than technical preferences.

Enterprise architecture is not about selecting products or designing networks. It is about enabling the organization to achieve its strategic objectives through technology. For this reason, every transformation initiative should begin with a clear understanding of the business vision, organizational priorities, operational challenges, and desired business outcomes.

The Strategy stage creates the foundation for the entire ENAF lifecycle by aligning business objectives with architectural direction before any technical design or implementation activities begin.

---

## Why This Stage Matters

Organizations often begin transformation initiatives by evaluating technologies before fully understanding the business problem they are trying to solve.

Questions such as:

- Should we migrate to the cloud?
- Should we adopt Zero Trust?
- Should we replace MPLS with SD-WAN?
- Should we modernize the data center?

cannot be answered without first understanding the business drivers behind the initiative.

Starting with technology frequently results in unnecessary complexity, increased costs, duplicated capabilities, and solutions that fail to deliver meaningful business value.

The Strategy stage ensures that architecture remains business-driven throughout the transformation journey.

---

## Business Focus

During this stage, the primary focus is understanding the organization rather than designing technology.

This includes understanding:

- Business vision and strategic objectives
- Organizational priorities
- Business capabilities
- Current business challenges
- Expected business outcomes
- Success criteria
- Business risks
- Constraints and assumptions
- Stakeholders and decision makers

The outcome is a shared understanding of why the initiative exists and what success looks like from a business perspective.

---

## Architectural Focus

Although no technical design work is performed during this stage, architectural thinking begins immediately.

Architects should identify:

- The business capabilities affected by the initiative
- Potential areas requiring architectural change
- Strategic dependencies
- Major transformation drivers
- High-level architectural considerations

These observations help prepare the next stage of the lifecycle without prematurely defining technical solutions.

---

## Expected Outcome

At the conclusion of the Strategy stage, the organization should have a clear and agreed understanding of:

- Why the transformation is required.
- Which business objectives will be supported.
- Which business capabilities are affected.
- Who owns the initiative.
- How success will be measured.
- Which constraints must be respected.
- Which assumptions have been identified.

This shared understanding provides the foundation for assessing the current environment and defining the future architecture.

---

## What This Stage Does Not Include

The Strategy stage intentionally avoids detailed technical activities.

It does not include:

- Product selection
- Vendor comparisons
- Solution architecture
- Network design
- Technology standards
- Implementation planning
- Configuration activities
- Deployment planning

These activities are performed during later stages of the ENAF lifecycle after the business direction has been established.

---

## Key Success Factors

The Strategy stage is considered successful when:

- Executive sponsorship has been established.
- Business objectives are clearly documented.
- Stakeholders agree on the scope of the initiative.
- Success measures have been defined.
- Business drivers are understood.
- Strategic priorities are aligned.
- The business case for change has been validated.

Once these outcomes have been achieved, the organization is ready to progress to the **Current State** stage of the ENAF lifecycle.
