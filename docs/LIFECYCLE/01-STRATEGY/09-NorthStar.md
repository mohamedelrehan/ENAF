# NorthStar Case Study – Strategy Stage

The NorthStar Retail Group case study demonstrates how the Strategy stage of the Enterprise Network Architecture Framework (ENAF) is applied in a real enterprise transformation initiative.

Rather than explaining the complete transformation, this document focuses only on the activities performed during the Strategy stage. The objective is to demonstrate how business needs are translated into a clear transformation direction before any assessment, architecture design, or technology selection takes place.

---

# Company Overview

NorthStar Retail Group is a multinational retail organization operating across Europe, North America, Asia-Pacific, and Latin America.

The company manages:

- Corporate headquarters
- Regional offices
- Manufacturing facilities
- Distribution centers
- Retail stores
- E-commerce platforms
- Regional data centers
- Public cloud services

Over the past decade, the company has expanded rapidly through acquisitions. As a result, the technology landscape has become increasingly complex, with different regional standards, duplicated infrastructure, inconsistent operational processes, and varying security practices.

Although the business continues to grow, the executive leadership recognizes that the current operating model will not effectively support future expansion.

---

# Business Challenges

During the initial workshops, business leaders identified several strategic challenges.

These included:

- Increasing operational costs.
- Slow onboarding of newly acquired companies.
- Inconsistent user experience across regions.
- Growing cybersecurity concerns.
- Limited operational visibility.
- Long deployment times for new sites.
- Difficulty supporting cloud transformation.
- Increasing complexity of the global infrastructure.

At this stage, these are considered business problems—not technology problems.

No architectural or technical solutions have yet been proposed.

---

# Executive Transformation Request

The Executive Board asks the Enterprise Architecture team to answer a fundamental question:

> **How should NorthStar evolve its enterprise technology capabilities to support business growth, improve operational efficiency, strengthen security, and simplify global operations over the next five years?**

The architecture team is asked to develop a business-driven transformation strategy rather than recommending specific technologies.

---

# Applying the Strategy Activities

The Enterprise Architecture team follows the ENAF Strategy methodology.

---

## STR-01 — Understand the Business

The team conducts a series of workshops with executive leadership.

Participants include:

- Chief Executive Officer
- Chief Information Officer
- Chief Financial Officer
- Chief Operations Officer
- Head of Retail Operations
- Head of Supply Chain
- Information Security Leadership
- Enterprise Architecture Team

The workshops focus on understanding:

- Corporate strategy
- Business vision
- Growth plans
- Business priorities
- Current operational challenges
- Strategic business initiatives

### Outcome

The architecture team gains a clear understanding of the organization's long-term direction before discussing technology.

---

## STR-02 — Identify Business Drivers

Using information gathered during the workshops, the team identifies the primary drivers behind the transformation.

Business drivers include:

- Continued international expansion
- Business acquisitions
- Digital transformation
- Cloud adoption
- Increasing cybersecurity expectations
- Cost optimization
- Operational simplification
- Improved customer experience

These drivers explain **why** change is required.

No assumptions are made regarding **how** the change should be implemented.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-02 Business Drivers Register

---

## STR-03 — Define Business Objectives

The business drivers are translated into measurable business objectives.

The Executive Board agrees that the transformation should aim to:

- Improve operational efficiency.
- Reduce operational costs.
- Improve business resilience.
- Strengthen cybersecurity.
- Support future acquisitions.
- Enable faster business expansion.
- Improve service availability.
- Simplify global operations.

Notice that these objectives describe business outcomes rather than technical implementations.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-03 Business Objectives Document

---

## Architecture Vision Workshop

With the business objectives agreed, the Enterprise Architecture team facilitates an Architecture Vision Workshop.

The purpose of the workshop is to define the characteristics of the future enterprise architecture without selecting specific technologies.

The agreed architectural direction is to create an environment that is:

- Secure by design
- Cloud-ready
- Globally consistent
- Operationally simple
- Scalable
- Resilient
- Highly automated
- Governed through common standards

At this stage, these are architectural principles rather than implementation decisions.

The selection of technologies will occur during later lifecycle stages.

---

## STR-04 — Define Scope

The architecture team defines the boundaries of the initiative.

### In Scope

- Enterprise connectivity
- Network services
- Security architecture
- Cloud connectivity
- Identity integration
- Operational governance
- Observability
- Business continuity

### Out of Scope

- Business application development
- ERP functional implementation
- End-user devices
- Manufacturing process redesign
- Office productivity platforms

A clearly defined scope ensures that the transformation remains focused on agreed business objectives.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-04 Scope Statement

---

## STR-05 — Identify Stakeholders

The architecture team identifies all stakeholders who will participate throughout the transformation.

Stakeholders include:

Business

- Executive Sponsor
- Business Owners
- Regional Leadership

Technology

- Enterprise Architecture
- Network Architecture
- Security Architecture
- Cloud Platform Team
- Infrastructure Operations

Business Support

- Finance
- Procurement
- Risk Management
- Compliance
- Project Management Office

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-05 Stakeholder Register

---

## STR-06 — Understand Financial Constraints

The Finance department participates in strategy workshops to establish the financial boundaries of the initiative.

The team documents:

- Available investment model
- Budget assumptions
- Cost optimization expectations
- Procurement considerations
- Financial risks

The objective is to ensure that future architecture decisions remain financially sustainable.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-06 Financial Assessment

---

## STR-07 — Assess Organizational Readiness

The Enterprise Architecture team evaluates whether the organization is prepared for a large-scale transformation.

The assessment considers:

- Existing skills
- Resource availability
- Governance maturity
- Operational capability
- Training requirements
- Executive sponsorship
- Change readiness

Several capability gaps are identified and documented for future planning.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-07 Organizational Readiness Assessment

---

## STR-08 — Identify Risks and Constraints

An initial strategic assessment identifies the major risks and constraints that may influence the transformation.

Examples include:

Risks

- Limited cloud networking experience
- Organizational resistance to change
- Budget uncertainty
- Regional operational differences

Constraints

- Existing contractual commitments
- Regulatory obligations
- Manufacturing availability requirements
- Limited specialist resources

These findings will be reviewed throughout the remaining lifecycle stages.

### Deliverable

DEL-STR-08 Initial Risk and Constraints Register

---

## STR-09 — Align Business and Architecture

The final activity validates that every architectural direction supports one or more agreed business objectives.

The Enterprise Architecture team confirms that:

- The transformation supports business growth.
- Strategic priorities have been addressed.
- Stakeholders are aligned.
- Scope is agreed.
- Financial expectations are understood.
- Organizational readiness has been assessed.

The Strategy stage is considered complete.

---

# Strategy Deliverables Produced

At the conclusion of the Strategy stage, the following deliverables have been completed.

- DEL-STR-01 Business Context Document
- DEL-STR-02 Business Drivers Register
- DEL-STR-03 Business Objectives Document
- DEL-STR-04 Scope Statement
- DEL-STR-05 Stakeholder Register
- DEL-STR-06 Financial Assessment
- DEL-STR-07 Organizational Readiness Assessment
- DEL-STR-08 Initial Risk and Constraints Register
- DEL-STR-09 Strategy Summary

These deliverables establish the business foundation for the remaining ENAF lifecycle.

---

# Decision Gate

The Enterprise Architecture team submits the Strategy deliverables to the Architecture Review Board for evaluation.

During Decision Gate **DG-STR-01**, the board confirms that:

- The business need is clearly understood.
- Business objectives have been approved.
- The scope is agreed.
- Stakeholders are identified.
- Financial considerations have been reviewed.
- Organizational readiness has been assessed.
- Strategic risks have been documented.
- Required deliverables have been completed.

The Strategy stage receives formal approval.

The project is authorized to proceed to the **Current State** stage.

---

# Preparing for the Current State Stage

Following approval of the Strategy stage, preparation begins for the Current State Assessment.

The architecture team plans the next phase by:

- Scheduling technical discovery workshops.
- Requesting existing architecture documentation.
- Identifying system owners and technical SMEs.
- Obtaining access to architecture repositories and operational tools.
- Preparing assessment templates.
- Planning site visits and interviews where required.

No technical assessment is performed during the Strategy stage; these activities prepare the team for the next phase of the ENAF lifecycle.

---

## Summary

The NorthStar Strategy case study demonstrates how an Enterprise Architecture team establishes the business foundation for a transformation initiative.

By completing activities STR-01 through STR-09, producing the required deliverables, and successfully passing Decision Gate DG-STR-01, NorthStar creates a shared understanding of the business objectives and strategic direction before progressing to the Current State stage.

The next phase will focus on understanding the existing environment and identifying the gaps between the current capabilities and the future business vision established during Strategy.
