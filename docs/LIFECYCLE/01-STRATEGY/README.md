# Strategy

The Strategy stage is the first operational stage of the Enterprise Network Architecture Framework (ENAF).

Its purpose is to establish a clear business foundation for an enterprise transformation before any assessment, architecture design, technology selection, or implementation activities begin.

Successful architecture starts with understanding the business—not the technology.

During this stage, Enterprise Architects work with business leaders and key stakeholders to understand the organization's vision, identify business drivers, define measurable objectives, establish the transformation scope, assess organizational readiness, and align the architecture initiative with the organization's strategic goals.

By the end of this stage, the organization has a shared understanding of **why the transformation is required**, **what success looks like**, and **what should be achieved** before moving into the Current State Assessment.

---

# Purpose

The Strategy stage ensures that every transformation initiative begins with a clear business direction.

Rather than focusing on technology solutions, this stage focuses on understanding the business, identifying the reasons for change, and creating alignment between business strategy and enterprise architecture.

This stage answers questions such as:

- Why is the organization changing?
- What business problems are being solved?
- What are the expected business outcomes?
- Who are the stakeholders?
- What are the business constraints?
- What does success look like?

---

# Stage Objectives

The objectives of the Strategy stage are to:

- Understand the organization's business strategy.
- Identify business challenges and transformation drivers.
- Define measurable business objectives.
- Establish the transformation scope.
- Identify stakeholders and governance responsibilities.
- Understand financial and organizational constraints.
- Assess strategic risks.
- Create alignment between business and architecture.
- Prepare the organization for the Current State Assessment.

---

# Learning Outcomes

After completing this stage, you should be able to:

- Explain the business reasons behind the transformation.
- Translate business strategy into architectural direction.
- Define clear business objectives.
- Establish project scope.
- Identify key stakeholders.
- Document strategic risks and constraints.
- Produce the required Strategy deliverables.
- Successfully complete the Strategy Decision Gate.

# Documents

The Strategy stage consists of the following documents.

| Document | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| 01-Purpose | Explains why the Strategy stage exists. |
| 02-Objectives | Defines the expected business outcomes. |
| 03-Inputs | Identifies the information required before the Strategy stage begins. |
| 04-Activities | Describes the work performed during the Strategy stage. |
| 05-Frameworks | Maps industry frameworks to the Strategy activities. |
| 06-Compliance | Identifies governance and compliance considerations. |
| 07-Deliverables | Defines the outputs produced during the Strategy stage. |
| 08-Decision-Gate | Defines the governance review required before progressing. |
| 09-NorthStar | Demonstrates the Strategy stage using the NorthStar case study. |
| 10-Checklist | Confirms readiness before requesting the Decision Gate. |

The documents should be completed in numerical order.

These deliverables provide the business foundation for the remainder of the ENAF lifecycle.

---

# Governance

The Strategy stage cannot be completed until the required governance activities have been performed.

The governance process consists of:

1. Completing all Strategy activities.
2. Producing the required deliverables.
3. Completing the Strategy Checklist.
4. Passing Decision Gate **DG-STR-01**.

Only after formal approval should the project proceed to the Current State stage.

# Next Stage

After the Strategy Checklist has been completed and **Decision Gate DG-STR-01** has been approved, continue to the **Current State** stage.

The Current State stage establishes the organization's baseline by assessing existing business capabilities, processes, applications, infrastructure, security, operations, and technology landscape.

Understanding the current environment is essential before defining the future Target State.
