# ENAF Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

The Enterprise Network Architecture Framework (ENAF) is committed to providing a professional, inclusive, respectful, and collaborative environment for everyone who participates in the project.

We welcome contributions from people with different experiences, backgrounds, industries, technologies, cultures, and levels of expertise.

All participants are expected to engage constructively and help maintain a community focused on learning, architecture excellence, and practical enterprise transformation.

---

## Expected Behaviour

Examples of positive and professional behaviour include:

- Treating others with respect and professionalism
- Providing constructive and actionable feedback
- Respecting different opinions, experiences, and approaches
- Focusing discussions on ideas, architecture decisions, and evidence
- Clearly explaining assumptions and design choices
- Accepting responsibility for mistakes and correcting them
- Giving appropriate credit to contributors and sources
- Protecting confidential, proprietary, and personal information
- Supporting collaboration and knowledge sharing
- Following applicable laws, standards, and project policies
- Prioritizing the long-term interests of the ENAF community

---

## Unacceptable Behaviour

Examples of unacceptable behaviour include:

- Harassment, intimidation, discrimination, or personal attacks
- Offensive, insulting, threatening, or degrading language
- Deliberately disrupting discussions or project activities
- Publishing private or confidential information without permission
- Misrepresenting another person's work as your own
- Plagiarism or removal of required attribution
- Submitting knowingly false, misleading, or unsafe technical guidance
- Promoting hidden commercial interests without disclosure
- Repeatedly ignoring maintainer guidance or project standards
- Abusing project communication channels
- Retaliating against anyone who reports a concern in good faith

---

## Technical and Professional Integrity

Because ENAF provides enterprise architecture guidance, contributors are expected to maintain a high level of technical and professional integrity.

Contributions should:

- Clearly separate facts, assumptions, opinions, and recommendations
- Avoid presenting vendor marketing statements as independent evidence
- Identify important risks, limitations, dependencies, and trade-offs
- Avoid including confidential customer, employer, or partner information
- Use fictional or anonymized examples unless permission has been granted
- Reference external frameworks, standards, and source material appropriately
- Avoid copying protected content without authorization
- Consider security, resilience, privacy, compliance, and operational impact
- Avoid intentionally harmful, insecure, or misleading guidance
- Remain vendor-neutral unless a document is explicitly vendor-specific

Contributors remain responsible for ensuring that their submissions do not violate intellectual property rights, confidentiality agreements, employment obligations, or applicable laws.

---

## Conflict of Interest

Participants should disclose any relevant relationship that could influence their contribution or recommendation.

This may include:

- Employment by a technology vendor
- Consulting relationships
- Commercial partnerships
- Sponsorships
- Financial interests
- Participation in competing frameworks or products

Having a commercial relationship does not prevent contribution. Transparency is required so that recommendations can be evaluated fairly.

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all ENAF project spaces, including:

- Repository issues
- Pull requests
- Discussions
- Reviews
- Documentation
- Community meetings
- Workshops
- Events
- Email and messaging channels
- Public communication made on behalf of ENAF

It also applies when an individual is officially representing the ENAF project in another community or public setting.

---

## Reporting a Concern

Concerns should be reported privately to the project maintainer.

When reporting a concern, include available information such as:

- What happened
- Where and when it happened
- Who was involved
- Relevant messages, links, or screenshots
- Whether the behaviour is continuing
- Any immediate safety or confidentiality concerns

Reports will be reviewed as fairly and confidentially as reasonably possible.

Do not publish sensitive reports in public repository issues.

A private reporting contact will be added before ENAF begins accepting broad public contributions.

---

## Enforcement

Project maintainers are responsible for interpreting and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Depending on the nature, severity, and frequency of a violation, actions may include:

1. Private clarification or guidance
2. A formal warning
3. Removal or rejection of content
4. Temporary restriction from project participation
5. Permanent removal from the project community
6. Escalation to the relevant platform, employer, organization, or authority when required

Maintainers may remove comments, commits, issues, pull requests, documentation, or other contributions that do not align with this Code of Conduct.

Enforcement decisions should be reasonable, consistent, and documented privately where appropriate.

---

## Good-Faith Disagreement

Professional disagreement is welcome and is an important part of architecture work.

Disagreement does not constitute misconduct when participants:

- Focus on the proposal rather than the person
- Explain their reasoning
- Consider evidence and alternatives
- Acknowledge risks and trade-offs
- Remain respectful
- Accept the final governance decision

ENAF encourages strong technical debate without hostility or personal criticism.

---

## Maintainer Responsibility

Maintainers should:

- Apply project rules consistently
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Protect confidential reports
- Provide clear reasons for important moderation decisions
- Correct mistakes when identified
- Encourage constructive participation
- Prioritize the integrity and long-term health of ENAF

---
